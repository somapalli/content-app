## üßæ **ADR: Merchant Pricing Calculation Strategy**

### **Context**

The platform must calculate **merchant pricing, fees, and adjustments** across authorization, clearing, and settlement for **all payment types**, supporting both **Blended** and **Cost-Plus** pricing models. The solution must reliably determine **gross and net amounts** while meeting **low-latency and high-availability** requirements for critical payment processing.

---

## **Option 1: In-House Pricing Calculation Engine with Cached Pricing from Zafin**

### **Description**

Build an internal pricing calculation engine that performs all fee, adjustment, and net/gross calculations in-house. **Zafin** acts as the **system of record** for contracts and pricing, with pricing data synchronized asynchronously and cached locally for runtime use.

### **Key Characteristics**

* Zafin stores contracts, pricing models, and fee configurations
* Pricing data is synchronized via scheduled or event-driven processes
* Local cache (e.g., in-memory / Redis) used for runtime pricing
* No real-time Zafin calls in the transaction processing path

### **Pros**

* ‚úÖ No external dependency in the critical processing path
* ‚úÖ Low latency and predictable performance
* ‚úÖ High resiliency (pricing available even if Zafin is unavailable)
* ‚úÖ Supports active/active and event-driven architectures
* ‚úÖ Business-friendly pricing management via Zafin

### **Cons**

* ‚ö†Ô∏è Additional complexity to build and maintain sync, cache, and validation logic
* ‚ö†Ô∏è Requires governance for pricing versions and effective dates
* ‚ö†Ô∏è Eventual consistency between Zafin and cached pricing

## **Option 2: External Pricing via Zafin**

### **Description**

All pricing and fee calculations are performed by Zafin at runtime. The platform invokes Zafin services in real time during authorization and settlement to retrieve calculated pricing results.

### **Key Characteristics**

* Zafin is responsible for pricing logic and calculations
* Real-time service invocation for each pricing request
* Minimal internal pricing logic

### **Pros**

* ‚úÖ Simplifies internal implementation
* ‚úÖ Centralized pricing logic and governance
* ‚úÖ Faster initial time to market
* ‚úÖ Business users can manage pricing with minimal engineering involvement

### **Cons**

* ‚ùå Introduces external dependency in the critical transaction path
* ‚ùå Adds network latency to high-volume payment flows
* ‚ùå Availability and performance tied to Zafin SLAs
* ‚ùå Requires strong resiliency patterns (timeouts, retries, fallbacks)
