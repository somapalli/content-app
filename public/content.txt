# **Technology Due Diligence – Merchant Ledger Solution**

---

## **1. Overview and Background**

As part of the merchant services modernization initiative, there is a critical need to establish a **robust ledger capability** to support end-to-end financial processing across the merchant ecosystem.

The merchant solution spans multiple financial flows, including **authorization, settlement, fee processing, funding, and reconciliation**, all of which require a **centralized, accurate, and auditable system of record**.

To address this need, we are evaluating and selecting the **appropriate technology solution for the merchant ledger** through a structured **technology due diligence process**. This process will ensure that the selected solution meets enterprise standards for **financial integrity, scalability, resilience, and compliance**.

---

## **2. What is the New Technology and What Will It Do**

The new technology under evaluation is a **modern ledger platform (sub-ledger)** designed to:

* Act as the **system of financial record** for all merchant transactions
* Support **real-time, event-driven financial posting**
* Enforce **double-entry accounting principles**
* Provide **idempotent and deterministic processing**
* Maintain an **immutable, auditable financial history**
* Enable **automated reconciliation** across systems
* Integrate with **funding systems and enterprise GL (e.g., Oracle)**

This is not a single predefined tool but a **technology capability area**, and due diligence will determine whether to adopt:

* An **in-house built ledger**,
* A **vendor-based solution**, or
* A **hybrid approach**

---

## **3. Why is the New Technology Needed**

The merchant solution **cannot operate effectively without a ledger capability**, as it is fundamental to:

* Tracking **all monetary movements**
* Ensuring **financial accuracy and balance**
* Supporting **merchant funding and payouts**
* Enabling **reconciliation across multiple systems**
* Providing **auditability and regulatory compliance**

### **Limitations of Current (Legacy) Systems**

* Primarily **batch-based**, lacking real-time visibility
* **Limited scalability** for growing transaction volumes
* **Fragmented financial tracking** across systems
* **Manual reconciliation processes**
* Insufficient **audit traceability**

👉 Therefore, a **modern ledger solution is required**, and due diligence is necessary to ensure the **right technology choice** is made.

---

## **4. Risks Associated with the New Technology**

Since the ledger is a **financial system of record**, selecting the wrong technology introduces significant risks:

| Risk Area                   | Description                      | Mitigation via Due Diligence         |
| --------------------------- | -------------------------------- | ------------------------------------ |
| Financial integrity risk    | Incorrect or unbalanced postings | Validate double-entry enforcement    |
| Duplicate postings          | Retry/replay scenarios           | Evaluate idempotency mechanisms      |
| Event consistency           | Out-of-order or missing events   | Assess event handling design         |
| Scalability limitations     | Inability to handle high volumes | Benchmark performance and scaling    |
| Integration complexity      | Multiple system dependencies     | Validate integration patterns        |
| Reconciliation gaps         | Incomplete financial matching    | Evaluate reconciliation capabilities |
| Audit/compliance risk       | Inadequate audit trail           | Ensure immutable ledger design       |
| Vendor risk (if applicable) | Lock-in, capability gaps         | Compare vendor vs build options      |

---

## **5. Background – Need for Merchant Ledger Capability**

The merchant ecosystem introduces **complex financial relationships and flows**, including:

* Multi-stage transaction lifecycle (auth → capture → settlement → funding)
* Multiple financial entities (merchant, processor, bank, internal accounts)
* Diverse financial events (fees, refunds, chargebacks, adjustments)

These require a **dedicated ledger capability** to:

* Maintain **accurate financial positions** across accounts
* Link **transactions to financial entries**
* Support **multi-layer reconciliation**
* Provide **real-time visibility into balances and liabilities**

Without a proper ledger, the system risks:

* Financial inaccuracies
* Reconciliation failures
* Operational inefficiencies
* Compliance issues

---

## **6. Objectives – Merchant Ledger Solution**

The objectives of introducing a ledger capability and conducting due diligence are:

* Identify and select the **most suitable ledger technology solution**
* Establish a **single, authoritative financial system of record**
* Ensure **accurate, balanced, and auditable financial postings**
* Enable **real-time financial processing and visibility**
* Support **high transaction scale and performance requirements**
* Provide **robust reconciliation and financial controls**
* Ensure **seamless integration with enterprise systems (GL, funding, reporting)**
* Minimize **technology and operational risks** through informed decision-making

---

## **7. Scope of Due Diligence**

The due diligence will focus on evaluating **technology options for implementing the merchant ledger capability**, covering:

### **Technology Evaluation**

* Build vs Buy vs Hybrid options
* Vendor capabilities (if applicable)
* Alignment with enterprise standards

### **Architecture & Design**

* Event-driven architecture (Kafka-based)
* Data model (ledger, accounts, transactions)
* Integration patterns

### **Financial Controls**

* Double-entry accounting
* Idempotent processing
* Posting rules engine

### **Scalability & Performance**

* Throughput and latency benchmarks
* Horizontal scaling capability

### **Resilience & Reliability**

* Failure handling and replay
* Active-active readiness

### **Reconciliation & Data Integrity**

* Automated reconciliation capabilities
* Break detection and handling

### **Security & Compliance**

* Access control and segregation of duties
* Audit and regulatory readiness

### **Operational Readiness**

* Monitoring, alerting, and observability
* Support and operational processes

---

## **8. How is Success Defined**

The due diligence will be successful if it results in:

* Selection of a **ledger technology solution that meets all functional and non-functional requirements**
* Demonstrated ability to ensure **financial accuracy and balance (double-entry)**
* Proven **idempotent and replay-safe processing**
* Ability to handle **required scale and performance**
* Strong **auditability and traceability**
* Seamless **integration with upstream and downstream systems**
* Clear **risk mitigation strategy for chosen solution**

---

## **9. How Will Due Diligence Results Be Measured**

The evaluation will be measured using:

### **Technology Fit**

* Alignment with architecture standards
* Flexibility and extensibility

### **Financial Accuracy**

* 100% balanced ledger entries
* Zero duplicate postings

### **Performance**

* Throughput (TPS / daily volume)
* Latency benchmarks

### **Resilience**

* Recovery and replay success rates

### **Reconciliation**

* Break rate and resolution time

### **Auditability**

* End-to-end traceability validation

### **Risk Assessment**

* Identified risks with mitigation strategies
* Vendor/technology risk scoring (if applicable)
